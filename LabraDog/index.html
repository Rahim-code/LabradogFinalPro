Some Facts About The Labrador
Retriever Breed

The sweet-faced, lovable Labrador Retriever is America’s most popular dog breed.
Labs are friendly, outgoing, and high-spirited companions who have a lot of
affection to suit a family looking for a medium dog.

Temperament: Friendly, Active, Outgoing

Breed Popularity: Ranks 1 of 200

Height: 22.5-24.5 inches (male), 21.5-23.5 inches (female)

Weight: 65-80 pounds (male), 55-70 pounds (female)

Life Expectancy: 10-12 years

Breed History

The Labrador Retriever is the traditional waterdog of Newfoundland, long
employed as a duck retriever and fisherman’s mate. The breed began its steady
climb to supreme popularity in the early 1800s, when Labs were spotted by
English nobles visiting Canada.

  • In 1892 two 'liver color' Labradors were born at Buccleuch's kennel.
  • In 1899, the first recorded yellow Labrador was born.
  • In 1903 the breed was recognized by the Kennel Club in England.
